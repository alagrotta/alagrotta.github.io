Wondering how many miles your car can go when the gas light comes on?

Follow the simple 3 steps below to find out how far!

Just enter your numbers after steps 2 & 3 and we'll calculate it for you!


The next time you're at a gas station, fill up a full tank of gas, and then RESET your trip meter.
This can usually be done by holding down the little peg next to the display.

Check your car's owners manual for more information on resetting the trip meter.


When your gas light comes on, stop at the next gas station and fill up your tank again.

Now divide the # of miles recorded on your trip meter by the # of gallons you just filled up (found on the receipt).

This will give your miles per gallon (MPG) since your last fill up from step 1. See example below:

Miles Since Last Fill-Up: (miles)
Gallons Filled at Station: (gal)
Ans: Miles Per Gallon (mpg)



Lastly, locate your car's tank capacity found in your owners manual, usually kept in the glove box.
Multiply the MPG found in step 2 times the difference in your tank capacity and
the amount of gallons filled up since the last trip. Or:

MPG from step #2: (miles/gal)
Tank Capacity: (gal)
Gallons Filled at Station: (gal)
Ans: Miles After Gas Light!

NOTE: This calculation is simply a best-estimate at your remaining milage once your gas light has come on.
Continually driving on too low of fuel can damage your fuel pump and should be avoided.
