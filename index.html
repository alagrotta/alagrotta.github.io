Wondering how many miles your car can go after the gas light comes on?

Follow these three simple steps below to find out how far!

Just enter your numbers after steps 2 & 3 and we'll calculate it for you!




The next time you're at a gas station, fill up a full tank of gas, and then RESET your trip meter.
This can usually be done by holding down the little peg next to the display.

Check your car's owners manual for more information on resetting the trip meter.





When your gas light comes on, stop at the next gas station ASAP and completely fill up your tank again.

Divide the # of miles driven since resetting the meter by the # of gallons you just filled up (found on the receipt).

This will give your miles per gallon (MPG) since your last fill up from step 1. See example below:

Lastly, locate your car's fuel tank capacity found in your owners manual, usually kept in the glove box.

Multiply the MPG found in step 2 times the difference in your tank capacity and
the amount of gallons filled up since the last trip:

Answer: Miles After Gas Light!

NOTE: This calculation is a best-estimate at your remaining milage once your gas light has come on. Continually driving on too low of fuel can damage your fuel pump and should be avoided.
